Why previous tools failed

5 constraints nobody had wired into the system

1
Data arrives at wildly different speeds
POS = 7-day lag. BTL = 21-day lag. Macro = monthly. Distribution = daily. When you mix these raw, your risk score is partly last week's picture and partly last month's. You're not looking at today.
2
Three tools, three contradictory answers
MMM says price elasticity = −1.4. Pricing Tool says −1.8. A 29% gap in elasticity means the two tools recommend different price actions. MMM shows distribution +12% contribution. The Distribution Planner shows −9.2% OOS drag right now. Nobody reconciles these.
3
Teams, backlogs and models are fragmented by market
Netherlands has 3 data scientists, all 3 models live. Poland has 0 data scientists, 118 backlog items, no models at all. Nigeria: 0 DS, 1 analyst, 203 backlog items. Recommendations written for NL will not run in PL.
4
Budget capped — can't rebuild everything everywhere
H1 2026 budget does not allow building an MMM from scratch in every market. You need a way to reuse what works in NL across similar markets — but only if the markets are genuinely similar, not just in the same region.
5
Country MDs want ROI they can verify themselves
When a recommendation says "fix OOS at Albert Heijn" the MD needs to see: how many cases, at what margin, over how many days, at what action cost. Not a model score. Not a probability. Bullet-point arithmetic they can challenge with a calculator.

Constraint 1 — Data freshness

The Super Agent: aligning 6 data streams to today

The pipeline does not average stale and fresh signals together. Instead, the Signal Normalization Agent identifies which signal is fresh, finds the historically-proven lead indicator for each stale signal, applies the regression-derived beta coefficient, and produces a probabilistic estimate of today's value — with confidence interval.

POS last reported 9,300 cases/wk 7 days ago
Distribution OOS: then → now 7.0% → 9.2% = +2.2pp Daily feed
Lead-lag beta (52-week history) −1.8% volume per 1pp OOS (r=0.85) Historical
Estimated POS adjustment −2.2 × 1.8% = −3.96% Derived
Normalised POS today ~8,932 cases/wk ± 370 [HIGH confidence] Agent output
5 Lead-Lag Relationships Encoded
Distribution → POS: lag 3.5d · r=0.85 · β=−1.8
ATL (GRP) → POS: lag 2.5wk · r=0.72 · β=+0.045
Pricing → POS: lag 0.8wk · r=−0.68 · β=−1.4
BTL → POS: lag 1.2wk · r=0.58 · β=+0.8
Macro → POS: lag 4.0wk · r=0.41 · β=+0.6
Confidence levels
HIGH (r ≥ 0.80): Distribution → POS
MEDIUM (r ≥ 0.60): ATL, Pricing
LOW (r < 0.60): BTL proxy, Macro extrapolation

BTL at 21 days stale → estimated via ATL direction (LOW). The agent recommends a mobile reporting form to cut the lag to 48h.
Why this matters
Without normalisation, the risk score is a mix of today's distribution data (good) + 7-day-old POS + 21-day-old BTL. The pipeline would fire an alert based on a signal that is, on average, 2 weeks behind. This agent is the difference between detecting a problem today vs. after it has already cost you cases.
Constraint 2 — Conflicting tools

Model Conflict Agent: 3 tools, 3 contradictions — here's what to trust

CONFLICT-1 Price Elasticity Magnitude HIGH · 29% divergence
MMM says
Elasticity = −1.4 (1% price rise = 1.4% volume loss)
Pricing Tool says
Elasticity = −1.8 (1% price rise = 1.8% volume loss)
Resolution: Use Pricing Tool (−1.8) for 12-week tactical decisions — it runs at weekly granularity with current pricing data. MMM's −1.4 reflects longer-run consumer adaptation. Both are correct for different time horizons. Trust winner: Pricing Tool
CONFLICT-2 Distribution Contribution HIGH · measures different things
MMM says (historical)
Distribution contributing +12% to volume (YTD channel expansion)
Distribution Planner says (today)
OOS rate 9.2% → active drag of −9.2% on volume RIGHT NOW
Resolution: Both are correct. MMM captures historical channel gains (positive, YTD). Distribution Planner captures current stock-outs (negative, this week). MMM runs monthly — it cannot see intra-week OOS events. Net real-time effect ≈ +12% − 9.2% = +2.8pp, not +12%. For this week's action: trust the Distribution Planner.
CONFLICT-3 MMM Forecast Accuracy — 15% gap HIGH · explained by blind spots
MMM predicted (4 weeks)
~39,200 cases
Actual (4 weeks)
~33,200 cases — −15.2% gap
Root cause: 3 MMM blind spots: (1) intra-week OOS not visible at monthly granularity, (2) competitor flash promos with <2-week notice, (3) distribution listing changes within month. Fix: combine MMM trend with Signal Normalization Agent for week-by-week accuracy.

Constraint 5 — Country MD wants to verify the numbers

Every recommendation: problem / the maths / ROI

No black boxes. No model scores. Every recommendation shows the arithmetic a Country MD can challenge with a calculator. Here is REC-001 in full:

PROBLEM
Albert Heijn will run out of Heineken in 6 days. Replenishment lead time is 4 days. Decision window: 2 days.
THE MATHS
Normalised sell-out rate 1,276 cases/day Signal Normalization Agent
Stock at Albert Heijn ~7,760 cases Distribution Planner (today)
Days of stock 7,760 ÷ 1,276 = 6.1 days Derived
Delivery lead time 4 days Logistics SLA
Decision window 6.1 − 4 = 2.1 days Derived
Do-nothing cost
~2 days × 1,276 cases/day
× €3.20 revenue/case
= €8,167 lost revenue
ACTION
Schedule extra delivery to Albert Heijn within 2 days. Switch from weekly to twice-weekly replenishment for 4 weeks.
Owner: Supply Chain Director · Deadline: 2 days
9x
13-week ROI
€8,167 protected ÷ €4,200 extra delivery
= 1.9x immediate
× 13 weeks sustained = ~9x quarterly
Why this format matters
The Country MD does not need to trust the model. They see: sell-out rate, stock on hand, delivery lead time, and the arithmetic to derive the decision window. They can call the supply chain director and say "Albert Heijn has 6 days of stock. Order placed?" Every number in this card can be verified by the MD in under 5 minutes.
